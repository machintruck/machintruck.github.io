Team CRH 2021

Posted by Arthur von Kaenel on Nov 20, 2022

CRH is HEIG's student robotics club. The team participates in the yearly Eurobots competition, a student autonomous robotics competition. I joined the team for the 2021 edition, when the theme was "Sail the world". Our robots had to navigate a 3x2 meter table filled with challenges based in a storm-wrecked harbor that the robots had to help fix. Challenges include interacting with the board's various elements, manipulating objects, and navigating around the table to correct positions.

Each team is allowed between one and two robots, with different size restrictions for each option. Our team opted for a dual robot strategy with two twin robots to minimize development time.

The competition

The competition is organized in a series of one on one matches, with two teams competing on the same table to gather the maximum amount of points in 100 seconds. Points can be obtained by doing the following actions: (The official rules can be found here.)

  • Organizing buoys on the corresponding colored side of either harbor.
  • Raising the fallen weather vanes.
  • Activating the lighthouse.
  • Parking on the side on the harbor indicated by the compass when the time ends.
  • Raising colored flags between the 95th and 100th second of the match.
  • Bonus points: correctly estimating the number of points gained during the match and displaying it on the robot.

Jack and Colombo's strategies

These are the names of our two twin robots. As part of our strategy, both prioritize big reward actions in the beginning of the round, concentrating on organizing only the most accessible buoys at the end of the runs. The competition is time-starved on purpose to encourage strategizing. A camera perched on the central mast helps to coordinate both robots.

Colombo is responsible for everything south of the main harbor. It's main task is to raise the weather vanes. To do so, a servo-activated arm deploys, and Colombo uses the full power of it's main wheel motors to swivel up the vanes. After doing so, it collects the five buoys next to the main harbor and deposits them by the secondary harbor. By the time it's done, time is up, so it returns to the correct side of the harbor to park.

Jack, by contrast, takes care of the north side. The most points it gains is by activating the lighthouse, which it does by using the same deploying arm as Colombo. Due to manufacturing difficulties, our team had to pivot on our lighthouse design. To provide a reliable last-minute result, a lego construction was made by team lego expert Alec.

Before activating the lighthouse, however, Jack picks up the five buoys held by the central mast. These are not team-coded, so getting to them fast before the other team potentially steals them is important. Once done with the lighthouse, it organizes them by color in the main harbor before finally parking on the correct side. An example of Jack's path can be seen in the embedded video.

Results of SwissEurobot

The entire SwissEurobot competition was live-streamed and can be accessed here. To even compete, the teams have to pass an approval phase. This consists of a referee validating that the robot follows the specifications, as well as a demonstrator match without an adversary. The robots have to execute a minimum of one action in the given time, as well as demonstrate adversary avoidance and the emergency button.

Of course, on the day of, a bug crops up in the system that makes the robots refuse to budge during the approval process. Thankfully, we can attempt approval as many times as necessary before the end of the approval period, and CRH was the first team to attempt the approval process. So after some head scratching the robots were moving again, and the competition was underway.

As competition went on, one thing was pretty clear: Jack and Colombo were far from the most performant robots. However, they were surprisingly consistent. While some teams managed to gain more points in a single match, their results fluctuated wildly from game to game. Our to robots always collected a majority of the points they could, and even had "perfect" runs for their programming. This lead to CRH defeating teams that in theory we could not. At the end of the day, we found ourselves in final.

We took home second place that day! This was an incredible end to a long and arduous journey that will stay with me forever. With only the slight grievance of my place in the final photograph (I'm behind everyone with the dark blue mask!).
